**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** February 2026
**Prepared by:** Fueld AI Ltd
**Purpose:** Guide logo design for the VoiceRails developer infrastructure platform

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**Name:** VoiceRails
**Domain:** voicerails.ai
**Tagline:** "Rails for voice AI. Ship production voice agents in minutes."

VoiceRails is a developer infrastructure platform that lets product teams build, deploy, and operate production-grade voice AI applications. It combines real-time voice model orchestration, telephony infrastructure, conversation workflows, and structured memory into one API layer.

The name carries two deliberate signals: **Voice** (real-time voice AI, speech, conversation) and **Rails** (infrastructure, tracks, speed, opinionated framework, the developer connotation from Ruby on Rails).

## 3. Logo Direction

### 3.1 Core Concept

The founder's direction: **A voice waveform pattern rendered through two parallel lines — channelling the "rails" metaphor.**

This is a strong concept because it unifies both halves of the name:
- The **wave pattern** = voice, audio, real-time speech
- The **two parallel lines** = rails, infrastructure tracks, stability, forward direction

### 3.2 Design Principles for the Mark

**Abstract and geometric.** Not a literal microphone, not a literal train track. The mark should feel like a technical diagram or signal pattern — something an engineer would appreciate.

**Two parallel paths.** The rails concept should read as two distinct lines running in parallel, with the voice waveform modulating their shape. Think of two signal traces on an oscilloscope that pulse with speech energy.

**Horizontal energy.** The mark should have a left-to-right directionality that implies speed, flow, and forward motion — like data moving through a pipeline.

**Asymmetric rhythm.** The waveform shouldn't be perfectly symmetrical. Vary the amplitude and frequency slightly to suggest organic speech patterns rather than a test tone. Real voice, not synthetic sine wave.

**Clean enough to work at 16px.** The mark needs to function as a favicon, a CLI icon, a GitHub avatar, and a nav logo. It must read clearly at small sizes.

### 3.3 Visual References (Mood, Not Copy)

- Sound wave oscilloscope traces with varying amplitude
- Railway track parallel lines with geometric rhythm
- Signal processing diagrams and spectrograms (abstract, not literal)
- The minimal geometric marks of Stripe, Linear, Vercel — technical and clean
- PCB trace routing patterns — parallel paths with purposeful direction

### 3.4 Mark Variations to Explore

1. **Waveform Rails:** Two parallel horizontal lines whose vertical position modulates like a voice waveform — they pulse closer and further apart as they travel left to right, forming a speech-energy pattern in the negative space between them.

2. **Signal Traces:** Two lines that start parallel on the left (calm/stable), then modulate through a voice-like wave pattern in the centre, and return to parallel on the right — representing signal in, voice processing, signal out.

3. **Abstract V:** The two rails forming a subtle "V" shape through their waveform modulation — nodding to "Voice" while maintaining the rails structure.

4. **Frequency Bars:** A minimal mark of parallel horizontal bars at varying heights, suggesting both a frequency spectrum and stacked infrastructure layers.

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Logotype (Wordmark)

The wordmark "voicerails" should be set in a geometric or grotesque sans-serif — clean, technical, and modern. Lowercase preferred to match the developer aesthetic (think how Vercel, Linear, and Supabase present).

Recommended exploration:
- **Instrument Sans** — geometric, slightly warm, pairs with the serif used on the website
- **DM Sans** — clean geometric, already used in the landing page body
- **Outfit** — geometric sans with good weight range, modern feel
- **Work Sans** — technical but friendly, good at small sizes

The "rails" portion of the wordmark can be differentiated with the accent colour (teal) to reinforce the two-word structure: `voice` + `rails`.

### 4.2 Monospace Accent

For secondary usage (CLI, docs, code contexts), the brand uses **JetBrains Mono** as a complementary typeface.

### 5.1 Primary Palette

| Background | Near-black | `#07080A` | Primary backgrounds, dark mode default |
| Surface | Dark charcoal | `#111318` | Cards, elevated surfaces |

| Text primary | Off-white | `#E8E9ED` | Headings, primary text |
| Text secondary | Cool grey | `#8B8D98` | Body text, descriptions |

### 5.2 Supporting Colours

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Provider: OpenAI | `#22D3A7` (teal) | Status, badges |
| Provider: Gemini | `#3B82F6` (blue) | Status, badges |
| Provider: ElevenLabs | `#EC4899` (pink) | Status, badges |
| Provider: Grok | `#F59E0B` (amber) | Status, badges |

### 5.3 Colour Principles

- **Dark-mode native.** The brand lives on dark backgrounds. The logo must be designed for dark-on-dark first, with a light-mode inversion available.
- **Teal is the signature.** `#22D3A7` is the brand colour. It should appear in the logo mark and differentiate VoiceRails from the sea of purple/blue AI brands.
- **Restrained palette.** The supporting colours are for UI context only. The logo itself uses teal + white/light grey only.

## 9. Summary for the Designer

VoiceRails is infrastructure for voice AI. The logo should feel like a **technical signal pattern** — two parallel lines modulated by voice energy. It should read as modern developer tooling: precise, confident, dark-mode native, with teal (`#22D3A7`) as the signature colour.
